    • Interesting Statistics

    • 70,000,000 Americans have some form of sleep disorder
    • Humans are the only mammal that voluntarily delay sleep
    • 37% of Americans have admitted to falling asleep at the wheel
    • The annual cost of Americans from lost productivity, medical expenses, sick leave, and property damage caused by sleep deprivation is $100,000,000,000

    Dangers of Sleep Deprivation

    • Impaired cognition
    • Affects cardiovascular health
    • Weight gain
    • Decline in performance
    • Higher level of anxiety and depression
    • Weakened immune system
    • Increased risk of diabetes
    • Lack of motivation

    Benefits of sleeping better

    • Memory improvement
    • Reduced chance of Alzheimer
    • Increased creativity
    • Longer lifespan
    • Increased athleticism
    • Sharpens attention
    • Lowers stress

    The Ideal sleep Environment

    • Maintain a clean and fresh sleeping environment by opening windows and changing sheets biweekly
    • Optimal room temperature is between 60-67 degrees fahrenheit
    • Remove excess light to enhance natural sleep
    • Remove the sources of sound from your environment

    Tips for a Healthy Sleep Cycle

    • Exercise 2-3 hours prior to bed
    • Limity interaction with any electronic devices because they have shown to emit wavelengths that suppress melatonin levels and leads to increased brainwave frequency.
    • Avoid stimulants (caffeine, sugar, tobacco, alcohol)
    • Keeping a consistent sleep schedule helps your sleep cycle
    • 7.5 hours of sleep is recommended to adults of all ages

    Fun Facts

    • You'll sleep better during a new moon and worse during a full moon.
    • If it takes you less than 5 minutes to fall asleep at night, you're probably sleep deprived.
    • Finding it hard to get out of bed in the morning is a real condition called dysania. It may signal a nutritional deficiency, depression, etc.
    • Being awake for 16 hours straight decreases your performance as much as if your blood alcohol leavel were .05% (legal limit: .08%).
